SDG 3: GOOD HEALTH AND WELL-BEING

PROBLEM BACKGROUND

The United Nations’ Sustainable Development Goal 3: Good Health and Well-Being focuses on promoting healthy
lives and well-being for all. Despite progress, health challenges persist everywhere. For example, the global
maternal mortality ratio was estimated at 197 deaths per 100,000 live births in 2023, though it is projected
to decrease with efforts in the health industry (World Health Organization, 2023). Another example is communicable
diseases such as AIDS and HIV, which continue to harm populations, with tuberculosis—one of the leading infectious
killers globally—causing 1.5 million deaths in 2020 alone (World Health Organization, 2021).

In the Philippines, the government has aligned its overall framework for health programs around SDG 3, but its budget
for public health expenditure has been cut by 3.5% (Healthcare Asia Magazine, 2025). The challenges of solving local health
problems persist today. One of these is dengue, a disease that has caused thousands of cases and hundreds of deaths in recent
years. From January to June 2025, there were 437 dengue-related deaths and 123,000 dengue cases locally (Sirbough, 2025).
These statistics show that, united, achieving good health and well-being heavily requires stronger healthcare systems,
preventive measures, community effort, and, most importantly, proper funding.

WHAT IS BEING DONE

Globally, the UN have created many programs worldwide that have improved the health of humans. Alongside
setting up goals to provide a framework for countries to build around. Countries such as the Philippines.
Where in the Philippines there are multiple efforts being done in order to try to help solve these problems today.
First, with programs,the Philippines quickly upgraded and improved its facilities as early as 2009 (Huntington
et al., 2011). Alongside new studies, the Philippines was able to decrease its maternal mortality percentage.
Second, the Philippines has partnered with the

CDC, which handles disease threats such as HIV and other communicable diseases, where they monitor and
try to prevent them from worsening. Alongside supporting each other to build capacity on monitoring, handling,
and responding to diseases (CDC in Philippines, 2024). Lastly, the Philippines, with the support of organizations,
placed its efforts to stop the spread of dengue, especially during rainy seasons. Using cleanup programs
to prevent dengue and other diseases (Sirbough,2025). Including an increase of 23.6 percent in healthcare
funding by the government.
